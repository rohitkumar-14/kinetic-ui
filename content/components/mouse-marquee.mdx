      </div>

## How it works

This component is incredibly fun to interact with. It tracks the global mouse position using `window.addEventListener('mousemove')` and normalizes the X coordinate between `-1` and `1` relative to the center of the screen.

- If your cursor is exactly in the middle of the screen (0), the marquee stops.
- If your cursor moves to the far right (1), the marquee scrolls quickly to the left.
- If your cursor moves to the far left (-1), the marquee scrolls quickly to the right.

The raw normalized value is passed through a Framer Motion `useSpring` so that jerky mouse movements don't cause the marquee to stutter, resulting in a buttery smooth scrub effect. When the mouse leaves the browser window, the spring gently settles back to 0.
