The Parallax Footer creates a beautiful premium effect where the main page content scrolls up to reveal the footer sitting underneath it, like a curtain being lifted. 

This is heavily used by top-tier creative studios and Awwwards-winning websites to add depth to the end of a page.

## Architecture

This component leverages `framer-motion`'s `useScroll` hook to precisely map the element's position relative to the viewport. As the container scrolls into view, the inner content translates downwards via a Y-offset, moving at a slightly slower rate than the actual scroll position. 

Combined with `overflow-hidden` on the wrapper, this creates the physical illusion that the footer is anchored behind the main content.
