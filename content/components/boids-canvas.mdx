## How it works

This component does not use WebGL or Three.js. Instead, it relies on an incredibly optimized 2D Canvas `requestAnimationFrame` loop.

The behavior of the "boids" (bird-oid objects) is governed by three simple mathematical rules coined by Craig Reynolds in 1986:
1. **Separation**: Steer to avoid crowding local flockmates.
2. **Alignment**: Steer towards the average heading of local flockmates.
3. **Cohesion**: Steer to move towards the average position (center of mass) of local flockmates.

Additionally, a fourth rule is injected into the velocity vector when the user's cursor comes within a certain radius, forcing the boids to rapidly scatter away from the mouse.
