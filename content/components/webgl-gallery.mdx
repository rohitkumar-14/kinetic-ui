title: WebGL Gallery
description: GLSL shader image gallery with chromatic aberration and liquid distortion transitions.

---

The `WebGLGallery` component is a drop-in replacement for any standard image slider. Instead of boring slide or fade transitions, it uses a **custom GLSL Fragment Shader** running on the GPU to liquify, tear apart, and RGB-split the current image as it transitions to the next one.

The shader pipeline combines four simultaneous effects:
1. **Chromatic Aberration** — Each RGB channel is sampled from a slightly different UV offset, creating the iconic "glitch" color splitting.
2. **Liquid Noise Distortion** — A procedural 2D noise function warps the UV coordinates, making the image appear to melt.
3. **Wave Displacement** — A sine wave ripples horizontally across the image during the transition.
4. **Dissolve Mask** — A noise-driven dissolve boundary creates a non-linear crossfade between the two images.

All four effects peak at the midpoint of the transition (`progress = 0.5`) and smoothly ease in and out.

## Installation

<CliInstallCommand componentName="webgl-gallery" />

> [!WARNING]
> **GPU Requirement:** This component runs a custom GLSL fragment shader on the GPU. It will not work on devices without WebGL support (extremely rare in 2025+). Performance is hardware-accelerated and typically runs at 60fps even on mobile GPUs.
