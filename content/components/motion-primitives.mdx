The `MotionPrimitives` file exports several lightweight wrapper components (like `<FadeIn>`, `<SlideUp>`, and `<ScaleIn>`) that make it incredibly easy to orchestrate standard UI entrance animations without writing verbose Framer Motion variants every time.

## Exported Components

All primitives accept standard `className` and Framer Motion `HTMLMotionProps` in addition to their custom props.

### 1. FadeIn
Standard opacity fade from 0 to 1.
- `duration` (default: 0.5): Length of transition.
- `delay` (default: 0): Wait before animating.

### 2. SlideUp
Translates upwards along the Y-axis while fading in. Uses a custom bezier curve for a snappy entrance.
- `yOffset` (default: 40): Starting position on the Y axis.

### 3. ScaleIn
Bounces from a smaller scale up to 1 using a backOut ease, creating a subtle pop effect.
- `initialScale` (default: 0.8): Starting scale percentage.

### 4. BlurIn
Transitions a CSS blur filter down to 0px while fading in. Excellent for typography or heavy images.
- `initialBlur` (default: "10px"): Starting blur amount.

### 5. RotateIn
Combines a slight rotation with scale and fade. Best used for floating cards or staggered grids.
- `initialRotation` (default: -15): Starting rotation in degrees.

### 6. StaggerContainer & StaggerItem
Use these together to orchestrate sequential animations for lists or grids.

- `<StaggerContainer>`
  - `staggerChildren` (default: 0.1): Delay between each child animating.
  - `delayChildren` (default: 0.1): Delay before the first child starts.

- `<StaggerItem>`
  - `yOffset` (default: 30): Starting position on the Y axis for the child slide-up animation.

## Viewport Logic

All motion primitives utilize `whileInView` instead of `animate`, meaning they will automatically trigger when they scroll into the viewport. They also use `viewport={{ once: true, margin: "-50px" }}`, meaning they wait until they are 50px inside the screen to trigger, and they will only animate once per page load.
